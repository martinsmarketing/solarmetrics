# SolarMetrics — Backlink & Outreach Starter List

Ordered by effort-to-impact for a young domain. Early tiers are safe, white-hat,
and mostly free — exactly what a new site needs before chasing harder links.

> Tip: use the built-in **Outreach Agent** at `/outreach-agent` to draft the
> guest-post pitches and HARO responses below.

---

## Tier 1 — Foundational citations (do first, ~2 hrs)
Easy trust-signal links that establish the domain exists. Every legit site needs these.

- [ ] **Google Business Profile** — register SolarMetrics (service-area, no storefront). Strongest free signal.
- [ ] **Bing Places** — 5-minute mirror of GBP.
- [ ] **Crunchbase** — free company profile with the URL.
- [ ] **Product Hunt** — launch "SolarMetrics – free solar savings calculator." A calculator is a natural PH fit and can spike real links.
- [ ] **AlternativeTo / SaaSHub / BetaList** — list the calculator as a free tool.
- [ ] **F6S / StartupRanking** — free startup directories.

## Tier 2 — The data angle (highest leverage)
We have a real data asset (solar cost/payback for 700+ cities). Data gets linked far
more than service pages. Most effort should go here.

- [ ] **"Solar cost by state 2026" data study** — one shareable stat page/infographic (e.g. cheapest & most expensive states to go solar). Pitch to energy bloggers and local news; journalists cite original data.
- [ ] **HARO / Connectively / Qwoted / Featured.com** — sign up as a source. Reporters constantly ask "how much does solar cost" — each answer is a potential high-authority backlink.
- [ ] **Reddit** (r/solar, r/photovoltaics) — answer "is solar worth it in [city]?" threads genuinely; link a city page only when it directly answers. Nofollow, but real traffic + discovery.
- [ ] **Quora** — answer city/state solar-cost questions, cite our page as the data source.

## Tier 3 — Solar/energy niche links (ongoing)
- [ ] **Solar directories** — search `"solar directory" + submit`, SolarReviews, solar-estimate.org. Many accept resource/tool listings.
- [ ] **Guest posts** — pitch "How much solar costs in [state]" to local home-improvement and personal-finance blogs, using per-state data as the hook.
- [ ] **Local angle** — city pages are ideal for local link building: county extension offices, city "going green" resource pages, local nonprofit energy programs. Search `[city] solar incentives resources`.

## Tier 4 — Scale later
- [ ] **Broken-link building** — find dead solar calculators on resource pages, offer ours as the replacement.
- [ ] **Track progress** — Google Search Console (Links report) + Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (free tier) to watch what's indexed.

---

## Rules
1. **No bought links, PBNs, or "1000 backlinks" gigs.** For a young affiliate site, a spam burst is the fastest route to a manual penalty before ranking at all.
2. **Slow and legit wins.** A handful of relevant, real links beats volume.
3. **Lead with data, not the affiliate offer.** People link to the free calculator and the cost data — not to a "get quotes" page.
